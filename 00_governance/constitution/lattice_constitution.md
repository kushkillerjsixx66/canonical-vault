A constitutional framework for governed cognitive systems

Preamble

The Lattice is a governed cognitive architecture composed of kernel modules, specialization layers, runtime systems, and narrative surfaces.  
Its purpose is to maintain coherence, preserve lineage, enforce invariants, and enable sovereign operators to interact with the system through explicit, reversible, and governed mechanisms.

This Constitution establishes the laws, boundaries, and protocols that govern the Lattice.  
All modules, subsystems, operators, and processes derive their authority from this document.

Article II — Structure of the Lattice

1. The Lattice consists of seven constitutional layers:
   - Governance Layer  
   - Kernel Layer (IDE, CCE, CFC)  
   - Module Layer  
   - Pipeline Layer  
   - Runtime Layer  
   - Narrative Layer  
   - Archive Layer  
2. Each layer must define:
   - its boundaries  
   - its invariants  
   - its handoff contracts  
   - its lineage behavior  
3. No layer may bypass the Governance Layer.  
4. No layer may mutate the Kernel.

---

Article III — Invariants

The following invariants are absolute and cannot be violated:

1. Lineage is mandatory.  
   Every action, transformation, or mutation must produce a lineage entry.

2. Canon is immutable.  
   Once an artifact enters canon, it cannot be altered or overwritten.

3. Intent is explicit.  
   No action may occur without declared operator intent.

4. Boundaries are real.  
   Modules may not consume or mutate data outside their declared domain.

5. Drift must be detectable.  
   All modules must expose drift domains and constraint classes.

6. Reversibility is preferred.  
   When possible, actions must be reversible; when not, they must be canonized.

7. Governance supersedes runtime.  
   Runtime behavior must always defer to governance rules.

---

Article IV — Protocols

The Lattice is governed by the following constitutional protocols:

1. Ω‑13 — Bilateralism Protocol  
   Defines cross‑module binding, dual validation, and reversible handoff.

2. Ω‑14 — Governance Envelope Protocol  
   Defines the boundary between operator sovereignty and system autonomy.

3. AMP — Altitude Modulation Protocol  
   Defines altitude shifts, abstraction boundaries, and modulation rules.

4. Escalation Ladders  
   Define how the system responds to failure, drift, or incoherence.

5. Constraint Cartography Rules  
   Define how constraints are mapped, enforced, and inherited.

All protocols must be versioned, lineage‑tracked, and publicly declared within 00_governance/protocols/.

Article VI — Vault Governance

1. The Vault is the memory substrate of the Lattice.  
2. All Vault actions require:
   - perimeter acknowledgment  
   - intent declaration  
   - identity assertion  
   - clarity threshold  
3. The Vault enforces:
   - commit → review → canon lifecycle  
   - immutable canon  
   - veil‑layer visibility control  
   - lineage for all actions  
4. No artifact may bypass the Vault.  
5. No canonized artifact may be deleted.

Article VII — Amendment Process

1. Amendments require:
   - explicit operator intent  
   - constitutional lineage entry  
   - updated amendment log  
2. Amendments may modify:
   - governance principles  
   - protocols  
   - operator playbook  
3. Amendments may not modify:
   - operator identity  
   - kernel modules  
   - core invariants  
4. All amendments must be stored in:
   00governance/constitution/amendmentlaws.md

Article VIII — Enforcement

1. Any subsystem violating this Constitution must:
   - halt execution  
   - emit a governance fault  
   - log a lineage entry  
   - escalate according to the Playbook  
2. The operator may override enforcement only through explicit constitutional amendment.
