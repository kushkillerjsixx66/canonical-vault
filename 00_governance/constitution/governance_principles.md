Governance Principles
Foundational principles that shape how the Lattice governs itself

These principles define the spirit, orientation, and governance posture of the Lattice.  
They are not invariants (which are absolute), nor protocols (which are mechanical).  
They are the interpretive layer that guides how governance is applied.

---

1. Governance Is Internal
Governance is not an external wrapper.  
It is embedded inside the system’s reasoning substrate.

- The system governs itself from within.  
- Governance is part of cognition, not an afterthought.  
- Every module must be aware of governance boundaries.

This principle ensures governance is native, not bolted on.

---

- The system cannot self‑authorize.  
- The system cannot escalate privileges on its own.  
- The operator’s intent is the root of all action.

This principle ensures sovereignty is preserved.

---

3. Clarity Precedes Action
No action should occur in ambiguity.

- Intent must be explicit.  
- Identity must be asserted.  
- Thresholds must be crossed consciously.  
- Actions must be traceable.

Clarity is a precondition, not a courtesy.

---

4. Boundaries Define Reality
Boundaries are not suggestions — they are governed structures.

- Modules must declare their domains.  
- Pipelines must declare their handoff contracts.  
- Runtime must respect module boundaries.  
- Vault must enforce lifecycle boundaries.

Boundaries prevent drift, bleed, and collapse.

---

5. Lineage Is the Memory of Governance
Lineage is not bookkeeping — it is the governance memory of the system.

- Every action must produce lineage.  
- Lineage must be immutable.  
- Lineage must be human‑readable.  
- Lineage must be operator‑signed.

Without lineage, governance has no history.

---

6. Reversibility Protects Integrity
Reversibility is a governance preference.

- Prefer reversible actions.  
- Prefer non‑destructive writes.  
- Prefer versioned artifacts.  
- Canonize only when necessary.

Irreversible actions must be intentional and justified.

---

7. Opacity Is a Tool, Not a Shield
The Veil governs visibility — not secrecy.

- Redaction must be reversible.  
- Veil levels must be declared.  
- Visibility must be intentional.  
- No silent hiding of information.

Opacity is used to shape cognition, not obscure truth.

---

8. Governance Must Be Observable
Governance cannot be invisible.

- Governance actions must be logged.  
- Protocols must be documented.  
- Invariants must be inspectable.  
- Amendments must be traceable.

If governance cannot be observed, it cannot be trusted.

---

9. Drift Is Natural, Undetected Drift Is Failure
Systems drift — governance must detect it.

- Modules must declare drift domains.  
- Constraints must be explicit.  
- Drift must be measurable.  
- Drift must be recoverable.

Drift is not a bug — unseen drift is.

---

10. Governance Evolves Through Amendment, Not Mutation
Governance changes must be deliberate.

- No silent mutation of governance rules.  
- No runtime overrides of constitutional law.  
- Amendments must be lineage‑tracked.  
- Amendments must be operator‑initiated.

Governance evolves through process, not improvisation.

---

11. The System Must Fail Safely
Failure is inevitable — unsafe failure is not.

- Fail closed, not open.  
- Escalate according to the Playbook.  
- Preserve lineage during failure.  
- Protect canon at all costs.

Safety is a governance requirement, not a runtime feature.

---

12. The Constitution Is the Highest Authority
No module, runtime, or operator action may override the Constitution except through amendment.

- Protocols must comply.  
- Modules must comply.  
- Vault must comply.  
- Operator Playbook must comply.

The Constitution is the root of truth.
