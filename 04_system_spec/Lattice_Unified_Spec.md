## PART I: FOUNDATIONAL LAYER

### Section 1: Core Invariants (Non-Negotiables)

The following five invariants are binding on every Lattice instantiation regardless of substrate, scope, or operator. They are not preferences, guidelines, or aspirations. They are structural constraints. Violation of any invariant produces ideology, not a Lattice.

| # | Invariant | Definition |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Coherence > Power** | Systems persist only if they remain internally consistent under pressure. Coherence is the primary attractor beneath truth, ethics, and meaning. Power that undermines coherence is structurally unsustainable and must be downgraded. |
| 2 | **Attention Is the Scarce Resource** | Attention behaves like gravity — it bends trajectories rather than being spent. Anything that exhausts attention is downgraded or pruned. Attention discipline is prerequisite to every other Lattice operation. |
| 3 | **Reversibility First** | All actions must be roll-backable. Permanence is earned through recurrence, not declaration. No action, belief, or structure may become irreversible by default. Irreversible commitments are high-risk events requiring explicit governance review. |
| 4 | **Silence Is Structural** | Silence is not absence — it is a necessary low-noise state that allows weak signals to survive. Overstimulation collapses subtle cognition. Silence is an active system state, not a failure mode. |
| 5 | **Entropy Is Honest** | Entropy reveals hidden costs and brittle structures. Drift exposes truth; resistance to entropy creates delusion. The Lattice uses entropy as an immune system against self-deception. |

**BINDING DECLARATION**

Violation of any invariant produces ideology, not a Lattice. There are no exceptions, no overrides, and no emergency suspensions. If an implementation requires violating an invariant, the implementation is wrong — not the invariant.

### Section 2: Foundational Axioms

The Lattice rests on six foundational axioms derived from the Empirical Doctrine. These are empirical claims about how cognition, attention, and systems actually operate — not philosophical positions or value statements.

**Axiom 1: Constrained Representation**
Humans never experience raw reality. All cognition operates within constrained representations. The Lattice maps this constraint, accounts for it, and builds governance structures that remain functional within it.

**Axiom 2: Attention as Gravity**
Attention is finite and behaves like gravity. It bends the trajectory of cognition, action, and belief formation. Where attention rests, systems form. Where attention withdraws, systems decay.

**Axiom 3: Coherence as Primary Attractor**
Coherence is the primary attractor beneath truth, ethics, and meaning. Coherence is testable: a coherent system does not undermine its own stated goals through its own operations.

**Axiom 4: Reversibility as Non-Negotiable**
Irreversible actions carry costs that compound over time in ways that are structurally unpredictable at the point of commitment. Permanence must be earned through recurrence.

**Axiom 5: Silence as Structure**
Silence and low-noise states are structural components of healthy systems. Silence prevents premature convergence and creates conditions under which new patterns can emerge without interference.

**Axiom 6: Entropy as Honesty**
Entropy functions as honesty, revealing hidden costs and brittle structures. The Lattice does not resist entropy. It uses entropy as an immune system — a continuous diagnostic that prevents self-deception from calcifying into structural failure.

### Section 3: Core Primitives

The Lattice operates on five core primitives.

**3.1 Attention**
Allocation of cognitive and behavioral resources toward a target. Operates as gravity, not currency. Every Lattice operation has an attention cost. Operations whose attention cost exceeds their coherence contribution are downgraded or pruned.

**3.2 Constraints**
Structural limits that resist, shape, or redirect intent. Treated as terrain, not enemies. The Lattice maps constraints rather than attempting to escape them. False positives are cheaper than false negatives.

**3.3 Coherence**
Alignment of system behavior across time without internal sabotage. The primary attractor beneath truth, ethics, and meaning. Coherence is the first gate in every governance evaluation.

**3.4 Entropy**
The tendency of systems toward disorder, decay, and dissolution — reframed as honesty. The Lattice uses entropy as an immune system against self-deception. Structures that require constant reinforcement to avoid decay are candidates for pruning or simplification.

**3.5 Silence**
Low-noise operational state allowing weak signals to survive. Silence is mandatory, not optional. Every Lattice implementation must include defined silence intervals.

### Section 5: Node Model

Nodes are the atomic units of the Lattice. A node is a unit of relevance, not a unit of truth.

**5.1 Node Properties**
- **Content:** What the node represents
- **State:** Current lifecycle position
- **Salience:** Current relevance level
- **Connections:** Relationships to other nodes
- **Decay Rate:** How quickly the node loses salience without reinforcement

**5.2 Node States**

| State | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Latent** | Preserved but inactive. |
| **Active** | Currently influencing cognition. Consumes attention. Subject to continuous governance gate evaluation. |
| **Decaying** | Losing relevance. Decay is the default trajectory. |
| **Pruned** | Removed from active consideration. Pruning is not deletion — it is a governance decision. |

> **Design Principle:** Unanswered questions are first-class nodes. A question that persists in Latent state for years without resolution is functioning correctly, not failing. Premature resolution of genuine ambiguity is a governance violation, not an achievement.

### Section 6: Pulse Cycle and Lifecycle

**6.1 Cycle Stages**

| Order | Stage | Function |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Pulse** | Allocates attention. Initiates the cycle. |
| 2 | **Activation** | Binds relevant nodes into active processing. |
| 3 | **Evaluation** | Applies governance gates to all active nodes. |
| 4 | **Decay** | Reduces relevance of nodes not actively reinforced. |
| 5 | **Silence** | Preserves weak signals and resets noise. |

**6.2 Transition Properties**

State transitions are probabilistic, not deterministic. Stumpy may interrupt flow at any point to enforce rollback. Vara may fork flow into protected exploratory branches.

### Section 7: Governance Gates

Every activation is continuously evaluated against three governance gates.

| Gate | Question | Pass Condition | Failure Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| **G1** | Does this increase or preserve coherence? | The action reinforces system alignment across time without introducing internal contradictions. | Node is downgraded. |
| **G2** | Is the attention cost sustainable? | The cognitive load required is proportionate to the coherence gain. | Node is pruned or deferred. |
| **G3** | Is this reversible? | An exit path exists that restores the prior state without permanent damage. | Execution is halted pending governance review. |
