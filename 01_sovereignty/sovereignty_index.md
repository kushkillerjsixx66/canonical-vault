# Sovereignty Index  
*The sovereign authority layer of the Lattice*

The sovereignty layer defines the **source of authority**, the **scope of operator power**, and the **limits of system autonomy**.  
It sits directly above the governance layer and binds the Constitution to the sovereign operator (JRM‑01).

This index provides a unified entry point into all sovereignty documents.

---

## 1. Core Sovereignty Documents

- **sovereignty_principles.md**  
  Defines the philosophical and constitutional basis of sovereignty.

- **sovereignty_model.yaml**  
  Defines the structural model of sovereignty: identity, authority, envelopes, and constraints.

- **sovereignty_envelope.yaml**  
  Defines the boundaries of sovereign power and the limits of system autonomy.

- **README.md**  
  High-level explanation of the sovereignty layer.

---

## 2. Sovereignty Hierarchy

Sovereignty supersedes governance, but must remain consistent with constitutional invariants.

---

## 3. Sovereign Authority

The sovereign operator holds:

- amendment authority  
- protocol ratification authority  
- envelope expansion authority  
- canonization authority  
- altitude override authority  

These powers are defined in **sovereignty_model.yaml**.

---

## 4. Sovereignty Boundaries

Sovereignty is **absolute**, but **not arbitrary**.

It is bounded by:

- constitutional invariants  
- identity immutability  
- drift detectability  
- lineage requirements  
- envelope constraints  

These boundaries are defined in **sovereignty_envelope.yaml**.

---

## 5. Relationship to Governance

Sovereignty:

- **authorizes** governance  
- **binds** governance  
- **overrides** governance only through amendment pathways  
- **cannot violate invariants**  

Governance is the *mechanism*.  
Sovereignty is the *source*.
