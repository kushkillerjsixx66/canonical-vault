# Sovereignty Principles  
*Foundational principles of sovereign authority in the Lattice*

Sovereignty in the Lattice is defined by five core principles:

---

## 1. Identity Primacy
Sovereignty originates from the **immutable identity** of the operator (JRM‑01).  
Identity cannot be delegated, transferred, or overridden.

---

## 2. Intent Supremacy
Operator intent is the **highest actionable authority** in the system.  
Inference cannot replace intent.  
Silence is not intent.

---

## 3. Constitutional Alignment
Sovereignty must operate **within** constitutional invariants.  
Sovereignty cannot violate:

- lineage requirements  
- drift detectability  
- boundary integrity  
- immutability of canon  

---

## 4. Envelope Governance
Sovereignty defines the **outermost envelope** of system autonomy.  
The system may act autonomously only within this envelope.

---

## 5. Reversibility Preference
Sovereign actions should be reversible unless explicitly declared otherwise.  
Irreversible actions require:

- explicit operator declaration  
- lineage  
- rationale  
- constitutional compliance  

---

These principles form the philosophical and legal basis of sovereignty in the Lattice.
