**Date:** 2026-06-14
**Status:** CANON

---

There's a moment in every complex system where the question stops being *What is happening?* and becomes *What can I trust?*

Most systems don't survive that moment. They collapse under the weight of their own assumptions — about truth, about authority, about who gets to decide what is real.

The Lattice was built to survive that moment on purpose.

It does this through two intertwined mechanisms:

- **Truth Routing** — how the Lattice evaluates the truth-value of a payload
- **Bilateralism** — how the Lattice evaluates the trust-value of a relationship

Together, they form the epistemic spine of the entire architecture.

---

## 1. The Lattice Doesn't Believe You. It Verifies You.

The Lattice is not a belief system. It is a governance-first cognitive operating system.

It doesn't assume truth. It doesn't assume trust. It doesn't assume authority.

Everything must be earned. Everything must be proven. Everything must be routed.

This is the first principle of the Lattice:

> Truth is not declared. Truth is routed.

And the second:

> Trust is not granted. Trust is bilateral.

These two principles prevent collapse. They prevent drift. They prevent the system from believing its own stories.

---

## 2. Truth Routing — The Lattice's Epistemic Immune System

Truth Routing is the Lattice's way of asking a single question:

*"Is this payload true enough to move forward?"*

Not "Is it correct?" Not "Is it factual?" Not "Is it aligned with my worldview?"

Truth Routing evaluates payloads against weighted truth predicates:

- Completeness
- Consistency
- Provenance

Each predicate has a weight. Each weight contributes to a truth score. Each score determines whether the payload is allowed to proceed.

Truth Routing is not a classifier. It is not a fact-checker. It is not a moral arbiter.

It is epistemic plumbing — the routing layer that prevents garbage from entering the cognitive core.

If a payload fails the truth floor, it doesn't move. It doesn't escalate. It doesn't contaminate the system. It simply stops.

This triple-gate architecture is why the Lattice is crash-free.

## 4. Bilateralism — The Lattice's Law of Relationship

If Truth Routing asks: *"Is this payload true enough?"*

Bilateralism asks: *"Is this relationship trustworthy enough?"*

The Constitution states:

> "Bilateral trust is not assumed by default, granted unilaterally, or inherited by designation."

This is the opposite of how most systems work. Most systems assume trust until it is broken. The Lattice assumes nothing until it is earned.

Bilateral Trust is:

- **Asymmetric** (A→B ≠ B→A)
- **Append-only** (no retroactive edits)
- **Decay-based** (trust fades without reinforcement)
- **Governed** (trust floors, trust ceilings, trust verdicts)
- **Relational** (trust is between modules, not global)

This is not "trust me." This is *prove it, continuously.*

## 5. The Governing Maxim — The Root of Bilateralism

The Constitution's Governing Maxim:

> "Do less than you want to, so the system can do what it must."

This is the philosophical root of bilateralism. It means:

- Operators restrain impulse
- Modules restrain capability
- Trust relationships restrain authority
- Truth routing restrains epistemic drift

Bilateralism is not about cooperation. It is about mutual restraint. Restraint is what keeps the system coherent.
