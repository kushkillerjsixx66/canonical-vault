# Empirical Doctrine  
*The supreme law of empirical truth in the Lattice*

The Empirical Doctrine defines the **rules of reality** for the Lattice.  
It governs how truth is established, how evidence is evaluated, and how empirical claims are validated.

This doctrine is constitutional‑class and cannot be overridden.

---

## 1. Jurisdiction

The Empirical Doctrine governs:

- evidence  
- signals  
- empirical claims  
- epistemic processes  
- drift detection  
- empirical decay  
- Vara integration  
- Stumpy integration  

---

## 2. Epistemic Invariants

1. Evidence must be classed.  
2. Signals must be qualified.  
3. Claims must be reversible unless declared otherwise.  
4. Drift must be detectable.  
5. Empirical decay must be measurable.  
6. No claim may bypass evidence.  
7. No signal may bypass qualification.  
8. No empirical state may mutate silently.  

---

## 3. Evidence Classes

Evidence is divided into:

- **Primary** — direct observation  
- **Secondary** — derived from primary  
- **Tertiary** — structural inference  
- **Quaternary** — narrative‑surface evidence  
- **Null** — unqualified or invalid evidence  

See: `evidence_classes.yaml`

---

## 4. Signal Qualification

Signals must be evaluated for:

- integrity  
- origin  
- drift  
- noise  
- reversibility  
- lineage  

See: `signal_qualification.yaml`

---

## 5. Empirical Decay

All empirical states decay unless:

- refreshed  
- revalidated  
- canonized  

Decay is measurable and must be tracked.

---

## 6. Vara Integration

Vara provides:

- signal decomposition  
- drift mapping  
- epistemic stress testing  
- substrate‑level introspection  

Vara outputs must be qualified before use.

---

## 7. Stumpy Integration

Stumpy provides:

- structural coherence checks  
- constraint mapping  
- empirical boundary enforcement  

Stumpy outputs must be classed as evidence.

---

## 8. Amendment Protocol

The Empirical Doctrine may only be amended through:

- explicit operator intent  
- constitutional compliance  
- lineage  
- doctrine‑class ratification  

---

## 9. Purpose

The Empirical Doctrine ensures:

- truth stability  
- epistemic coherence  
- evidence integrity  
- safe reasoning  
- governed empirical behavior  

It is the **supreme empirical law** of the Lattice.
