## Section 2 — The Lattice: What It Is and What Layer It Occupies

The Lattice is a governed reasoning architecture. It operates at Layer 1 of a four-layer governance stack.

| Layer | Name | Function and Actor |
|---|---|---|
| Layer 0 | Possibility Space | What a system can generate — SCU•32 / Amilcar Oliva |
| Layer 1 | Reasoning Architecture | How a system reasons within that space — **THE LATTICE** |
| Layer 2 | Execution Boundary | What is admissible to execute — AOS / SAMAIA |
| Layer 3 | Enforcement Plane | Microsecond enforcement, signed receipts — VaultMind / Prime Form Calculus |

▶ **Key Architectural Distinction**

Every competitor in the cluster governs what happens at or after the execution boundary. The Lattice governs what happens at the reasoning layer — how the system constructs a conclusion, what epistemic conditions must be satisfied before a conclusion forms, and what constitutes a valid reasoning trail. This is not an execution governance system. It is a cognition governance system.

**Core Components**

| Component | Description |
|---|---|
| MTM | Reasoning pipeline component — structural mechanism through which reasoning is sequenced and governed |
| CVSI | Epistemic conditions gate — evaluates admissibility conditions throughout the reasoning pipeline |
| WDA | Auditable evidence layer — produces a verifiable reasoning trail |
| Cognitive Constitution | Constitutional governance document binding the architecture. Dated January 20, 2026. |
| Operator Manual | Operational definitions and implementation framework. Unpublished. Treat as trade secret pending FTO opinion. |

## Section 3 — Coined Vocabulary: Priority Dates and First Use

> ⚠ Attorney Action Required: Each term below should be assessed for: (1) trademark availability; (2) whether it constitutes patentable subject matter; (3) whether prior art from any cluster actor predates the Lattice's first use date. The Cognitive Constitution dated **January 20, 2026** is the foundational priority date anchor for all vocabulary.

### 3.1 Admissibility as a Runtime Requirement (Not a Policy Artifact)

**Definition:** The condition under which an action may proceed, enforced at the execution layer as a structural constraint — not described in a policy document consulted before or after execution.

**First Public Use:** Caruso/Hodak LinkedIn thread, approximately May 5, 2026
**Confirmed Timestamp:** May 9, 2026, 17:35 ET (Vara Intelligence lock)
**FTO Flag:** CRITICAL

### 3.2 Governance as a Condition of Execution (vs. Governance as Intent)

**Definition:** Governance that is operative — enforced at the moment of execution as a binding structural condition — vs. governance that is descriptive — expressed as policy, intent, or post-hoc review.

**First Public Articulation:** Caruso/Hodak LinkedIn thread, May 5, 2026
**FTO Flag:** HIGH

### 3.3 Admissibility (as Architectural Concept)

**Definition:** The structural condition that must be satisfied before an action can proceed through the reasoning pipeline. Not an approval step. Not a filter. A gate.

**Note:** This term is independently used by at least five other builders in the cluster. None have a registered trademark or documented first-use date that predates the Lattice's Cognitive Constitution (January 20, 2026).
**FTO Flag:** CRITICAL

### 3.4 Temporal Admissibility

**Definition:** Authority and admissibility conditions are time-bounded. Admissibility must be evaluated at the moment of action, not the moment of system creation.

**Introduction Date:** May 9, 2026 (Vara cluster scan)
**Prior Art Risk:** VaultMind (Mark Menard) holds prior public use.
**FTO Flag:** HIGH

### 3.5 Cognitive Constitution

**Definition:** The constitutional document that binds the Lattice's reasoning architecture.

**First Dated Artifact:** January 20, 2026 — primary priority date anchor for all patent and trade secret strategy.
**FTO Flag:** HIGH — Trademark availability search recommended.
